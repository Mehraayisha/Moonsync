Menstrual Health Support Knowledge Base
What is a menstrual cycle?

The menstrual cycle is the monthly process in which the body prepares for pregnancy. A typical cycle lasts between 21 to 35 days. Bleeding usually lasts 3 to 7 days.

Common symptoms before and during periods (PMS)

Before periods, many people experience:

Mood swings

Bloating

Breast tenderness

Headache

Tiredness

Cramps in lower abdomen
These symptoms are caused by hormonal changes and are normal for many individuals.

Period cramps and relief

Cramps happen due to contraction of the uterus. To reduce cramps:

Use a heating pad on the lower abdomen

Drink warm fluids

Do light stretching or walking

Rest properly

Stay hydrated

Irregular periods

Periods may become irregular due to:

Stress

Poor sleep

Sudden weight changes

Hormonal imbalance

Excessive exercise

If periods are missed for more than 2–3 months, medical advice is recommended.

Heavy bleeding

Heavy bleeding may be identified if:

Pads/tampons need changing every hour

Period lasts more than 8 days

Large blood clots are present
Medical consultation is advised in such cases.

Hygiene during periods

Change pads every 4–6 hours

Wash hands before and after changing

Keep the genital area clean and dry

Dispose used products properly

Mood changes during periods

Feeling sad, irritated, or emotional before periods is common due to hormonal shifts. Relaxation, sleep, and self-care can help manage mood changes.

Food and hydration

During periods:

Drink plenty of water

Eat iron-rich foods (spinach, dates, legumes)

Avoid excessive caffeine and junk food

Warm foods may provide comfort

Exercise and periods

Light exercise such as walking or yoga can reduce cramps and improve mood. Heavy workouts may be avoided if feeling tired.

When to consult a doctor

Medical advice should be taken if there is:

Severe abdominal pain

Fainting or dizziness

Very heavy bleeding

No periods for 3 months

Periods before age 8 or not started by age 16

Tracking periods

Tracking helps to:

Predict next period date

Understand cycle pattern

Identify irregularities early

Emotional support

Periods can feel uncomfortable and tiring. Rest, self-care, and talking to someone supportive can help. Experiencing discomfort during periods is common and manageable.

PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome)

PCOS is a common hormonal condition that can affect menstrual cycles. It may cause:

Irregular or missed periods

Weight gain

Acne or oily skin

Excess facial hair

Difficulty in ovulation

Lifestyle habits such as balanced diet, regular exercise, and stress management may help. Medical consultation is important for proper diagnosis and care.

PCOD (Polycystic Ovarian Disease)

PCOD is related to hormonal imbalance where ovaries produce immature eggs. It may lead to:

Irregular periods

Weight gain

Hair thinning

Acne

Maintaining healthy lifestyle and seeking medical advice is recommended.

Endometriosis

Endometriosis occurs when tissue similar to the uterine lining grows outside the uterus. It may cause:

Severe period pain

Pain during periods or bowel movement

Heavy bleeding

Difficulty in daily activities during periods

Medical attention is strongly advised if such symptoms occur.

Fibroids

Fibroids are non-cancerous growths in the uterus. Symptoms may include:

Heavy or long periods

Pelvic pain or pressure

Frequent urination

Lower back pain

Consult a doctor if these symptoms are experienced.

Thyroid and periods

Thyroid hormone imbalance can affect menstrual cycles. It may cause:

Irregular periods

Very light or very heavy bleeding

Fatigue and mood changes

A thyroid check-up can help identify the issue.

Anemia due to heavy periods

Heavy periods over time can lead to anemia. Signs include:

Weakness

Dizziness

Pale skin

Shortness of breath

Iron-rich foods and medical consultation are important.

When symptoms are not normal

Seek medical advice if there is:

Severe pain that stops daily activity

Bleeding lasting more than 8 days

Missing periods for 3 months

Sudden change in cycle pattern
